Complex Task Scenario: Automated Water Delivery

Nova is designed to handle intricate multi-step tasks, freeing your loved ones from daily chores. Consider the scenario of fetching a water bottle from a remote dispenser:

1. Voice Command & Planning

Grandparent says: "Nova, please bring me a water bottle from the dispenser." Nova processes the Telugu command, identifies the intent, and plans the optimal route to the known water dispenser location, which might be up a garden hill.

2. All-Terrain Navigation

Nova autonomously navigates the terrain, utilizing its tracked chassis and terrain-stabilizing actuators to climb the $45^{\circ}$ hill. LiDAR and IMU SLAM ensure precise localization and obstacle avoidance even on uneven ground.

3. Smart Dispenser Interaction

Upon reaching the dispenser, Nova's vision system identifies the dispenser's interface. Its hydraulic arm precisely extends to place a pre-programmed RFID card or activate a button, triggering the water pouring mechanism.

4. Bottle Retrieval & Securing

After the water pours into a designated bottle (perhaps one Nova carried or a fixed one at the dispenser), Nova's soft-padded gripper securely grasps the filled bottle, ensuring no spills.

5. Safe Return & Delivery

Nova then plans its return journey, carefully descending the hill and navigating back to the grandparent's location, using its internal memory to recall preferred delivery spots. It announces: "Your water is ready."

6. Contextual Learning

The robot logs the successful task, updating its internal map with information about the dispenser and terrain conditions, improving its efficiency for future requests.

Mechanical Systems

Nova’s physical structure is a blend of industrial robustness and home-friendly design. It features a high-torque tracked chassis for mobility and a powerful hydraulic arm for heavy lifting, all balanced by dynamic stabilizers, crucial for navigating varied home and garden environments.

Key Components & Capabilities

  • ●
    Chassis & Mobility: 40cm diameter aluminum base with four 250W brushless DC motors driving rubberized tracks. Engineered for climbing $45^{\circ}$ slopes and traversing low obstacles with ease.
  • ●
    Hydraulic Arm: A 1500N linear actuator capable of lifting 100 kg up to 2m. The soft-padded gripper is designed for both heavy water bottles and delicate interaction like card placement.
  • ●
    Stabilizers: Four 900N linear actuators dynamically extend to counterbalance loads and level the chassis, ensuring stability on uneven terrain and during heavy lifts.
  • ●
    Internal Cargo Bay: A 40cm diameter bay designed to securely stow a 20L water bottle or equivalent payload.

AI & Perception Pipeline

Nova's intelligence stems from a modular AI stack running on an NVIDIA Jetson Orin NX. Its primary language for interaction is **Telugu**, ensuring seamless communication with your grandparents. This pipeline processes sensor data to understand its environment, interpret complex commands, and respond intelligently, enabling tasks like smart dispenser interaction.

Perception Module

This module is Nova's "eyes and ears," converting raw sensor data into meaningful information about the world, crucial for navigating diverse environments and interacting with objects like a water dispenser.

  • Object Detection: Uses a YOLOv8 model, accelerated by TensorRT, to identify objects like water bottles, furniture, and even specific dispenser interfaces from a 4K camera feed in real-time.
  • Label Reading (OCR): Detected objects are cropped and passed to PaddleOCR to read text on labels (e.g., dispenser instructions) or even recognize specific cards for interaction.
  • Mapping (SLAM): Fuses data from LiDAR and an IMU to build a 3D map of its environment, allowing for autonomous navigation to known locations (like the water dispenser) and precise localization on varied terrain, including hills.

Language & Logic Module

This module interprets human intent and translates it into actions, enabling natural interaction and complex task execution for the grandparent assistant.

  • Speech-to-Text (STT): An offline Vosk ASR model recognizes voice commands in Telugu, allowing grandparents to give commands without needing internet access.
  • Natural Language Processing (NLP): A fine-tuned TeluguBERT model parses the recognized text to classify user intent (e.g., "fetch water," "emergency stop") and extract parameters like "from the dispenser" or "place the card."
  • Text-to-Speech (TTS): Uses Coqui TTS or eSpeak to generate synthesized Telugu speech for responses and confirmations, providing clear feedback to the user.
  • Decision Logic: A high-level task scheduler in ROS2 receives the parsed command and orchestrates the required actions, from planning a path to controlling the arm for precise dispenser interaction.

Control Systems & Navigation

Advanced control theory ensures Nova moves smoothly and safely, especially under heavy loads and on challenging terrain like hills. It fuses data from multiple sensors for robust localization and precise navigation, essential for complex tasks like dispenser interaction.

Core Control Principles

  • PID & Adaptive Control: Each motor and actuator uses a PID controller for precise position and speed. The system dynamically adapts to heavy loads and changing terrain (e.g., hill climbing) by monitoring current draw and adjusting gains, ensuring smooth and stable motion.
  • Load Balancing & Stability: When the arm extends with a heavy payload (like a full water bottle) or on an incline, the IMU detects any tilt. The stabilizer actuators automatically extend and retract to shift the center of gravity and prevent tipping, maintaining equilibrium on slopes.
  • ROS2 Runtime: All software modules run as nodes in a ROS2 environment. This provides robust, real-time messaging, scheduling, and fault tolerance for the entire system, crucial for coordinating complex multi-step tasks.
  • Terrain Response: Nova adjusts its behavior based on ground contact and incline. If the IMU senses a steep slope, the speed of the tracks is reduced to maintain optimal traction and prevent slipping.

Sensor Fusion with Kalman Filter

For robust SLAM and localization, Nova combines asynchronous data streams using an Extended Kalman Filter (EKF). This ensures accurate position tracking even when individual sensors might be unreliable.

Power & Electronics

A robust power system is critical for safely delivering high currents for lifting and providing long runtimes, especially for extended tasks like fetching water from a distant dispenser. Nova uses a high-capacity LiFePO₄ battery and a custom Power Distribution Board (PDB) with multiple safety features.

Power Distribution

The main 24V battery power is efficiently stepped down to different voltage rails for various subsystems, ensuring stable and reliable power delivery.

Battery Performance vs. Load

Understanding how different operational loads impact battery life is crucial for planning tasks and ensuring Nova can complete its duties without interruption.

Key Power & Electronic Features

  • LiFePO₄ Battery: A 150 Ah, 24V custom pack chosen for its high cycle life, thermal stability, and safety, providing up to 14 hours of operation.
  • Smart BMS: Monitors cell voltages, currents, and temperatures, with an emergency contactor for immediate power cut-off on fault.
  • Dual-Redundant Power Rails: Critical circuits (motors, arm) are fed by parallel 24V rails to prevent single-point failures.
  • Custom 4-Layer PCB: Integrates regulators, connectors, and microcontrollers with isolated analog/digital grounds and enhanced creepage distances for safety.
  • Microcontroller Co-processing: STM32 and ESP32 handle time-critical I/O and networking, offloading the Jetson Orin NX.
